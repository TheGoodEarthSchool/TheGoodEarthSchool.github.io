Little Green Heroes Suitable for ages 4 to 5

Understanding our environment and what children can do to care for it.

Workshops in this series

  • Guided Nature Walks
  • Beach and Urban Cleanups
  • Biodiversity Workshops, in-centre or external
  • Plastic Recycling Workshop
  • Urban Tiller Workshop

Some Programmes

Guided Nature Walks

In this workshop, children will learn about:

- Local biodiversity in Singapore
- Basic plant and animal identification
- The sounds and textures of nature
- How to respect and protect natural spaces

Please note:

Our guided walks explore the ecosystems and wildlife found in natural spaces, but sightings of specific animals cannot be guaranteed.

Wildlife visibility depends on factors such as the weather, time of day and animal behaviour. The session focuses on habitats, biodiversity and respectful observation.

The unpredictability of nature is part of the learning experience.

Beach and Urban Cleanups

In this workshop, children will learn about:

- Local biodiversity and its connection to clean habitats
- The impact of litter on marine and urban wildlife
- Ways to reduce waste and recycle effectively

Activity highlights include:

- A hands-on cleanup at a beach or urban area
- Sorting collected litter into recycling and general waste
- Team activities that build responsibility and cooperation

Plastic Recycling Workshop

In this workshop, children will learn about:

- The importance of recycling to protect ecosystems and wildlife
- How plastic affects biodiversity and food chains
- Simple ways to reduce plastic use

Activity highlights include:

- Safe hands-on activities using recyclable materials
- Creative reuse activities using plastic items
- Group discussions about plastic waste and recycling

Urban Tiller

In this workshop, children will learn about:

- The basics of urban gardening
- The value of green spaces in cities
- How plants support biodiversity and reduce urban heat
- How to care for plants

Activity highlights include:

- A planting activity where children pot a plant
- Simple games about farming and plant care
- Discussions about water, sunlight and soil

Biodiversity Workshops

In this workshop, children will learn about:

- The importance of wildlife within ecosystems
- How humans and wildlife can live together in urban areas
- Responsible actions that support local wildlife
- How different species interact
- Why natural habitats should be protected

Activity highlights include:

- Interactive sessions using animal plushies and specimen models
- Hands-on activities about ecosystems and food chains
- Role-playing activities about animal behaviour and needs
